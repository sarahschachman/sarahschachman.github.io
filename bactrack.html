BACtrack

Overview

May 2015

BACtrack helps students drink safer during a night out. Binge drinking is one of the biggest issues on college campuses, and the problem with binge drinking is that it’s hard to know when you’ve had enough until it’s too late. Making smart decisions begins with being informed. BACtrack allows you to track your BAC (Blood Alcohol Content) throughout the night, as well as track trends in your drinking habits to help you stay safer in the long run.

Deliverable

This app is a final project I created for my iOS Development course. It was written from scratch in Swift and is fully functioning.

How it works

During a night out, you can record your drinks to monitor BAC levels to help you stay at a good level. At the beginning of the night you simply begin a new session, and add drinks as you consume them. Every time you have a drink, it will be added to your current location on the map representing your night out. Meanwhile, the app will calculate your current blood alcohol content (BAC). This is calculated based upon elapsed time and your weight, which is set in settings. Your BAC will be displayed alongside information about safe BAC levels, and if you are reaching your limit. This will help you monitor how much alcohol you are intaking so that you don’t reach dangerous levels. It will also keep track of where you have each drink, so you can look back on a map of the night. This is helpful as a tool for tracking patterns in drinking habits, and seeing which locations you drink the most. The "Add Drink" page is super easy to use because it is designed with the distracted user in mind. It needs to require as little thought as possible, so that the user continues to add drinks in accordance with consumption throughout the night.

Next Steps

If I had time to really develop this idea and give it more comprehensive features, these are a few things I would add:

  • The ability to have the app text a designated friend if your BAC is reaching dangerous levels.
  • A detailed report about your habits so that you can keep tabs on your drinking.
  • The ability to share your location with a designated friend.

Revel

Overview

Fall 2014

Despite the multitude of exciting formal events and parties on campus, most college students don't want to be (and cannot afford to be) buying a brand new dress for each occasion. Before every formal, my friends and I recieved many emails over our sorority lists requesting dress swaps, or to borrow a dress for the event. Nobody wants to keep wearing the same dresses, but we don't want to buy brand new ones that we'll wear once; many people resorted to asking their friends to borrow. We saw a space that was in dire need of innovation. People trust their friends, and the proximity of your friends on campus makes a college-wide dress swap a lucrative idea. We wanted to create a platform to aggregate dresses that people were willing to lend (for a little reward), and for people to come looking for a "new" dress to wear. This idea was spearheaded by my friend Lily, a fashion advocate and board member of Harvard Women in Business. I was recruited to do the front-end development and design of the platform. The app was entered into the annual Harvard Innovation Challenge and unfortunately it did not win, but we are proud of how it turned out -- it was a great learning experience for all of us!
